Hardware

This page summarizes the physical hardware platforms used to evaluate TACTIC, including the whole-arm exoskeleton interface and distributed tactile skin sensor mounts used for contact-rich human–robot interaction.

Exoskeleton

The whole-arm exoskeleton platform enables safe, high-bandwidth physical interaction and provides proprioceptive measurements used during contact-aware control. The system supports multi-link contact and is integrated with vision and tactile sensing for hybrid predictive control.

Skin Sensor Mounts

Distributed skin sensor mounts provide dense tactile feedback across the robot arm, enabling detection of contact location, normal direction, and force magnitude. These measurements are critical for predicting and regulating interaction forces during whole-arm pHRI.
